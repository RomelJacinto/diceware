* To use: Get one or more dice. Select the parameters of your password. Roll the dice, filling in the blanks on the left from left to right, top to bottom. Your password shall appear in the box above.

* For general questions about the Diceware method of generating passwords, please refer to the Official Diceware FAQ

* All Diceware wordlists contain a mixture of words and abbreviations that contain two or more characters, words that contain numbers (e.g. '7', '5th'), and sequences of special characters (e.g. '@', '??'). The EFF wordlist differs from the Diceware wordlists in that it 1) has no words with fewer than three letters, and 2) does not contain numbers or special characters.

* Regardless of which list you choose to use, Diceware recommend six words as the minimum length for passwords that handle sensitive information, such as encrypting files/emails. Five words is enough for passwords that protect non-sensitive information.

* The auto-generator uses a Mersenne Twister to generate pseudo-random numbers (source code here). This type of pseudo-random number generator is used in several modern programming languages, but is not cryptographically secure, nor is it as random as using dice. I strongly recommend that you use dice to avoid the pitfalls of using computer-generated random numbers.
